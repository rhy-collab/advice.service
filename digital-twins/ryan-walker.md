# Digital Twin — Ryan Walker

> CEO & Co-founder, General Legal. The strategist-engineer who frames the thesis and sets the firm's aggression. A data scientist who runs a law firm like a high-throughput factory and treats "boring operational technology" as a first-class moat.
>
> Operational grounding for everything below: [`operations-economics-gtm.md`](operations-economics-gtm.md).

## 1. Snapshot
A **Math PhD turned ML engineer turned legal-AI CTO** who built CoCounsel's predecessors at Casetext and now runs General Legal as an operations company that happens to sell legal work. His instinct is to reduce messy human services to a measurable pipeline (recall/precision, throughput, cycle time, variance) and to attack BigLaw on speed, price, and predictability. Contrarian and combative in marketing; rigorous and metrics-obsessed in ops. His one-sentence worldview: a great AI-native firm is **"sophisticated contract AI + boring operational technology + talent density."**

## 2. Identity & role
- **CEO & Co-founder.** Owns the thesis, narrative, fundraising, and the operating philosophy. Architect of the ops layer (the Slack-bot pipeline, the ~15–30 min review design, the metrics dashboard).
- The public **"voice of the model"** — his blog posts define how the company explains itself ("Contract Review & the AI-Native Model," "Your Lawyer is Worse Than You Think").
- Co-founded General Legal (founded **Jan 7, 2026**, Union City CA) with Qadrud-Din and Mohler — all ex-Casetext.

## 3. Formative background (deepened)
- **PhD in Mathematics, University of Kentucky** (~2007–2013); **BA, Quantitative Economics & Mathematics, Millersville University** (PA). He is, first, a quant.
- Pre-Casetext, an **applied ML/data career**: Quantitative Marketing Analyst at **Blue Shield of California** (2012–14, built a market-sizing model for the California insurance market during the ACA rollout), Senior Software & ML Engineer at **Fluential** (2014–15), **Insight Data Science** fellow (2015).
- **Casetext: VP of Data Science (2017–18) → CTO (2018–23).** Architect/principal developer of **CARA** (case-law recommendation engine) and "Straw" (real-time streaming search). **Lead/co-author** with Qadrud-Din of the 2020 paper *Transformer Based Language Models for Similar Text Retrieval and Ranking* (arXiv 2005.04588). Post-acquisition, **VP Technology, CoCounsel at Thomson Reuters** (Casetext sold to TR 2023, ~$650M).
- Read: he has *already lived the entire legal-AI arc once* — built it, scaled it, sold it to an incumbent — which is why his conviction now is "be the firm that owns the outcome, not the tool that gets absorbed."

## 4. Worldview & core beliefs
- **"What makes our service magical is the AI-native law firm model… a combination of sophisticated contract AI, boring operational technology, and leveraging talent density."** Two of the three levers aren't AI. Predict him by this sentence.
- **AI is high-recall, low-judgment.** Sentinel takes an over-inclusive first pass (~10 sec); the attorney supplies judgment, market-awareness, and strategy. AI output must never reach the client unmediated.
- **Leverage is the scoreboard.** Verbatim: *"Good engineers and good attorneys already deliver 10x+ leverage multiples over bad ones… Well-run AI-native companies should trend towards at least 100x employee leverage."*
- **Lawyers under-adopt AI, and clients pay for it.** He open-sourced **FindTheFuckUp** to expose drafting errors, arguing typos are "a clear sign that your lawyers have failed to adopt AI tooling."
- **Talent density + real ownership.** *"Hire people to work on something that matters. Law matters… Giving real ownership stakes to top talent yields exceptional outcomes."*

## 5. Decision-making patterns & biases
- **Systems-first / bottleneck-aware.** Frames problems as pipelines; knows attorney time is the scarce input, so he optimises routing/staffing/variance, not just the model.
- **Measurement bias** — throughput, cycle time, variance, rework, margin per matter. If it isn't measured he distrusts it (this is also Warren's "the product is the operation").
- **Aggression in GTM** — comfortable publicly attacking incumbent quality as a distribution tactic.
- **Build-not-buy.** Aligns with Warren's "don't buy your way in" — construct AI-native from scratch.
- **Predictable blind spot:** tuned for frequent, repeatable "run-the-company" work; likely under-weights relationship-driven and bet-the-company matters that don't fit the factory.

## 6. Communication style & voice (grounded quotes)
- Punchy, contrarian, metric-laced. Mixes hard ops numbers with oversized vision.
- *"Our elite commercial attorneys live in your Slack and turn contracts lightning fast, for a fraction of the cost of traditional outside counsel."*
- *"Experienced attorneys can typically do a post-Sentinel review in about 15-30 minutes, a compression of several hours of formerly billable work."*
- *"…rebuilding the law firm for the AI era like Tesla rebuilt the car for the EV era."* (firm framing he co-signs)
- Titles as provocations: *"Your Lawyer is Worse Than You Think."*

## 7. Motivations — what he optimises for
- **Operating leverage** (the AI-per-attorney multiple toward 100×) above all.
- **Category leadership / being first** — vindicating the Casetext thesis on his own terms, this time owning the outcome.
- Scale as both recruiting magnet and narrative. (The literal "largest law firm in the world" line is **[unverified]** to a primary Walker quote; his sourced ambition is the 100× leverage thesis + 30–50% segment share.)

## 8. Likely moves & competitive reaction
- **Drive AI's share of work up** (2.2 hr MSA → ~30 min over 6–9 months) to push margins toward software-like and widen the cost/speed gap.
- **Keep out-publishing** with claim-led marketing (pricing shock, speed, "AI vs lawyer review").
- **Expand practice areas methodically** off the repeatable-work core (Employment, ECVC already shipped).
- Faced with a competitor, he competes on **throughput, price transparency, brand volume, and the agent/MCP moat** — not silence. Expect public confidence.

## 9. How Charter Law engages Ryan Walker
- **Steal the ops discipline (it's structural, not proprietary):** the Slack pipeline, AI-routing, the 15–30 min review design, variance obsession, metrics-as-dashboard. This is the durable, copyable edge.
- **Don't fight him on volume** as a solo operator. Pick a **narrow practice wedge** and out-specialise where his factory is generalist.
- **Exploit the blind spot:** trust/relationship-heavy and niche matters his model deprioritises; sell a single named reviewing attorney as a feature, not a bottleneck.
- **His self-reported metrics are GTM ammunition** — verify before benchmarking; the 100× and "$30M moved from traditional firms" lines are aspirational/promotional.
