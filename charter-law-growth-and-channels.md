# Charter Law — Growth & Channel Strategy

> Why General Legal made **LinkedIn ads** their primary acquisition channel, whether Charter Law should, and how a business like this actually wins. Pairs with `charter-law-ad-library.md` (the 71 ads) and `charter-law-video-scripts.md`.
> Researched 30 June 2026. Status: DRAFT v1.

## 1. Why LinkedIn ads — and why it fits this business specifically

General Legal pours creative into a disciplined LinkedIn ad system (71 live ads) for reasons that map almost perfectly onto a founder-targeted legal service:

**1. It's the only platform that targets *who someone is at work*.** LinkedIn lets you target by **job title, seniority, company size, industry, and even named accounts — simultaneously** — using data people *self-report* on their profiles. Google targets what someone is *searching*; Meta targets *interests/demographics*. For Charter Law, the buyer is precise — **founders, COOs, heads of ops, finance leads at growth-stage startups** — and LinkedIn is the only channel that can put an ad in front of exactly those people before they go looking.

**2. B2B ROI is genuinely better here.** 2026 benchmarks put LinkedIn at ~**1.8× ROAS for B2B vs Google's ~1.25×** (≈44% more revenue per dollar), and **LinkedIn leads convert to opportunities at 2–3× the rate** of other social platforms. Cost per *ICP account engaged* is ~**$257 vs Google's ~$560**.

**3. It "creates demand" instead of just capturing it.** Most founders aren't Googling "flat-fee contract review" — they don't know it exists. LinkedIn lets you **interrupt them with the pain** ("deals stall when contracts stall") *before* they search. That's why General Legal's ads are pain-led, not keyword-led.

**4. Bigger, better deals.** LinkedIn-sourced B2B deals run ~**28–35% larger** because you reach senior decision-makers directly — and in legal, the decision-maker (the founder) is also the buyer.

**5. The medium suits the message.** Their whole pitch is "BigLaw quality, founder-native." Showing up professionally in a founder's *professional* feed reinforces credibility in a way a Meta/Instagram placement wouldn't.

> The honest trade-off: **LinkedIn is expensive.** CPCs run ~$5–8; **cost-per-lead typically $80–200** (Meta is 3–5× cheaper per click for raw reach). You pay a premium for precision and lead quality. That premium is rational for a high-value service (a $500–$2,000 matter, repeat relationship) but it shapes *how* a bootstrapped Charter Law should sequence things (§4).

## 2. The channel roles (don't think "one channel" — think a funnel)

The best B2B advertisers in 2026 combine platforms by job:

| Channel | Job | Charter Law use |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn ads** | **Create demand** — reach ICP founders before they search | Primary paid channel; the 71-ad system |
| **Google ads** | **Capture demand** — catch the few actively searching ("startup contract lawyer," "MSA review") | Small, high-intent search campaign later |
| **Meta ads** | **Retarget** warm audiences cheaply | Retarget site visitors / video viewers once you have a pixel |
| **Organic LinkedIn + founder network** | **Free demand** — content, warm intros, communities | **Where a solo founder should start** (§4) |

## 3. How a business like this actually *wins* (the strategic picture)

The research is blunt: **building the AI is no longer the moat — distribution is.** Foundation models made the tech easy; everyone's product looks similar. So the durable advantages for an AI-native legal service are:

1. **The human-in-the-loop *is* a moat.** Counterintuitively, the licensed-attorney layer — harder to scale, harder to copy, and the thing that builds trust — is a genuine competitive advantage, not just overhead. It's also your **regulatory moat** (you need a bar license to give legal advice; a competitor can't vibe-code around it).
2. **Brand & trust.** Selling *outcomes* (not software) requires customers to extend trust. In professional services, brand has always been the moat. Charter Law's name, voice, and consistency matter.
3. **The compounding data moat.** Every matter improves your playbooks and your sense of "what's market." Over time you know more about how *these* contracts get negotiated than anyone starting fresh — and switching costs rise because you hold the client's history. (This is the moat that *grows* with volume.)
4. **Distribution that's hard to rent.** Paid ads can be copied by anyone with a budget. The defensible layers are **a trusted brand, owned content/audience, warm referral networks, and workflow embed** (being *in* the client's Slack). Build those alongside the ads.
5. **Land narrow, expand by client pull** (from the operating playbook): win one repeatable job, then grow inside the trusted channel. Retention + expansion beats endless top-of-funnel spend.

**The synthesis:** ads get the first contract; the **attorney-backed outcome, the brand, the accumulating playbook, and the in-channel relationship** keep the client and grow the account. Spend on ads to *start* relationships; win on trust and consistency to *keep* them.

## 4. What Charter Law should actually do (solo, bootstrapped — sequence matters)

General Legal runs heavy paid LinkedIn because they have ~$12M. A solo, pre-revenue Charter Law should **sequence**, not copy the spend:

**Stage 1 — Free distribution first (pre- and early-revenue).** Before paying $80–200/lead, exhaust the channels that cost time not money — exactly the first-10-clients motion in the roadmap:
- **Founder network + warm intros** (your strongest early channel).
- **Organic LinkedIn content** in your own voice — the "AI prepares, an attorney decides" POV, the pain stories, the pricing transparency. (Your competitor's whole *brand* is built on LinkedIn content; you can do the organic version for free.)
- **Startup communities / accelerator Slack groups / fractional-CFO & advisor referrals.**
- Goal: land the first 3–10 paying matters and gather **real proof** (testimonials, turnaround data) — which is exactly what your ads will later need to be truthful.

**Stage 2 — Turn on paid LinkedIn, small and measured (once you have proof + a little budget).**
- Use **LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms**, not just external landing pages — they cut CPL ~**25–35%** with pre-filled fields and convert at 8–15%.
- **Target tightly:** founders/ops/finance, company size ~11–200, in your served regions. (Targeting senior C-suite raises CPL 40–60%, so test broader operator titles too.)
- Start with the **day-one-truthful families** from the ad library (flat-fee, pricing-shock, BigLaw contrast, quality, general commercial); hold the attorney-claim and social-proof families until those claims are true.
- Run the **A/B plan**: find the winning *family* first, then test hook → format → offer → CTA, judged by **cost-per-booked-call**, not vanity metrics.

**Stage 3 — Build the funnel around the ads.**
- **Google** search campaign for the high-intent few; **Meta** retargeting for site/video viewers (cheap warm touch); a clean **landing page or Lead Gen Form** that the ads point to (the ads are only half the funnel — the conversion page matters as much).

**Budget reality:** at ~$100 CPL and, say, a 20–30% lead-to-paid rate, a first paid matter costs roughly **$300–500 in ad spend** — so a $500 matter barely breaks even on *acquisition* alone, and the economics only work on **repeat business and expansion**. That's the whole reason Stage 1 (cheap/free channels) and retention matter so much for a bootstrapped start.

---

## 5. The one-paragraph answer to "why LinkedIn, and how do we win?"

General Legal chose LinkedIn because it's the only channel that can put a pain-led ad in front of *exactly* the right person — a growth-stage founder — before they even know your service exists, and those leads are higher-value and higher-converting than anywhere else (worth the premium price). But ads only *start* relationships. The way Charter Law actually wins is the part competitors can't rent: a **licensed attorney standing behind every outcome** (trust + a regulatory moat), a **brand and voice** founders recognise, a **playbook that compounds** with every matter, and **living inside the client's workflow** so they expand with you. For a solo, bootstrapped start: **win the first clients through your network and organic content, gather real proof, then layer on tightly-targeted paid LinkedIn** — and judge it on cost-per-booked-call and, above all, on repeat business.
